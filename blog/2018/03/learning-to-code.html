LEARNING TO CODE

22 March 2018 by DZEIG

Thinking about enrolling in a coding bootcamp?

I attended the General Assembly Bootcamp, and in my experience, GA is/was an amazing boot camp. I learned a ton, I met wonderful people, created a lot of interesting things, and I had a lot of fun during my time there. I'm still in touch with some of the people from my cohort and we've all grown in our own ways throughout the intensive course. These are all things you may be able to get from other bootcamps as well, so do your own research and see what you think might be better suited for you.

That being said, upon enrolling in a boot camp, if you are unprepared there is a lot that can wrong, and fast.

I recommend checking out freeCodeCamp as a way of getting started. It will help you figure out if you want to even attend a boot camp. They touch on most of the things you'll learn at a bootcamp and more(like algorithms, some coding bootcamps don't spend time teaching algorithms). They help you build out your portfolio by assigning projects. freeCodeCamp is also completely free of charge, as it in a non-profit organization.

The only downsides that I have found with freeCodeCamp is that, although it is self-paced, it lacks human interaction, no one is holding you accountable, making it easy to give up if you feel frustrated. However, freeCodeCamp makes up for this by presenting you with the opportunity to join groups and attend meet-ups. If these downsides don't sound like they'd affect you, then by all means stick to freeCodeCamp. You'll save yourself a lot of money in the long run.

If you decide to go to a boot camp here are some things that you'll need

A hiatus:
However long the length of the boot camp, should determine the length of your hiatus. When attending GA's orientation, everyone was told that this will literally consume our lives. Like most people there, I thought it was exaggerated. Turns out they weren't exaggerating. The hiatus is important.

Time to decompress:
I cannot stress this enough. Throughout the course, you are going to process tons of information and can become easily overwhelmed. If you do not have a method of decompressing, things can start to go south. I recommend doing yoga and meditation. Something to get your mind off of the course for at least an hour. It can make all the difference.

Sign up for meetup.com and attend a few meetups centered around what you are interested in learning. Meetups/tech events aren’t actually boring. They can be pretty fun, intense, and a great experience. Peruse the site and see what you can find. It’s gonna be a long, difficult but fun journey. Good luck!
